The Hotel

The Golden Lion Hotel features a popular public bar, or if you're looking for some peace and quiet we also have a private bar for residents. Featuring a silver service restaurant where you can eat while enjoying a wonderful sea view, there's no need to go far in order to enjoy a relaxing stay. However should you wish to venture further, here's just a taste of what the local area has to offer.

Shopping

Hunstanton features a small, bustling High Street with a mix of local and chain stores. From picking up those holiday essentials, to souvenirs or that special boutique purchase, there's something to suit everyone.

Eating Out

As a seaside town, local cuisine tends toward seafood with a number of highly rated 'Fish and Chip' restaurants, the majority of which also provide a takeaway service allowing you to enjoy your food outside on sunny days.

There are however other options, particularly in the Northgate Arcade which boasts a number of world foods from Chinese dishes to Indian specialities.

Bars

There are plenty of places to go and enjoy a drink in the town and no matter whether your preference is for the traditional pub, the modern bar or the family friendly establishment there will be somewhere to suit.

With some bars open into the early hours and featuring music or live bands, you can choose to dance the night away.

Attractions

Donkey Rides

Located just off the central promenade, the traditional donkey rides have been a staple attraction for many years. With experience handlers, both children and adults can enjoy a ride along the beach on either a donkey or pony.

Rainbow Park

Situated just back from the left promenade, a permanent fair has sat on this site for over 50 years, with fairground rides having been a feature of the town since 1880. Arcades, Waltzers, Dodgems, a traditional Helter Skelter and even a Ghost Train means there's plenty of fun to be had at the fair!

Sealife Sanctuary

Located right on the seafront, the Sealife Sanctuary offers the opportunity to get up close and personal with a number of creatures that you might meet in the sea (and a few you might not want to!).

Featuring diver's eye viewing tanks, a touch pool and and underwater tunnel, the centre combines a fun day out with educational experience.

There is also a seal hospital located at the centre, where feeding displays take place as well as regular talks.

Sea Tours - Seal Island

Voted the top visitor attraction on Trip Advisor, Searles Sea Tours invite you to board their amphibious vessel at the central promenade, with a choice to venture out to 'Seal Island', one of the numerous common seal colonies in the wash, to view the seals in their natural habitat. Alternatively sail along the coast on one of several tour options, enjoying the panoramic views whilst listening to experienced local guides detailing the exciting and varied history of the area.

Oasis Leisure Centre

Equipped with a full fitness suite, heated pool and outdoor lido as well as offering roller skating, bowls, squash and a soft play area for toddlers, the Oasis Leisure Centre is the ideal place for family fun, or to keep up that gym schedule while you're away.

The Princess Theatre

Originally opened as a cinema in 1932, The Princess Theatre offers a variety of entertainments from plays to dance shows and film screenings to music events. Also the veranda offers a fantastic view of the green and coastline, particularly in the evening as the sun sets over the sea.
